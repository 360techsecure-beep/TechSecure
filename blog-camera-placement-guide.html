Where to Place Security Cameras on Your Home: An LA Homeowner's Guide

The most expensive camera in the world is useless if it's pointed at the wrong spot. After hundreds of installs across Los Angeles, we've learned that good placement matters more than fancy hardware. Here's where to focus.

The front door

Roughly a third of break-ins happen right through the front door, and it's also where deliveries land. This is the single most important camera on the house. Mount it to capture faces and the porch, not just the street.

Driveway & garage

Your driveway is where cars, bikes, tools, and people transition onto your property. A camera here documents vehicles (and plates) and covers the garage — a common entry point people forget to secure.

Back and side doors

Intruders prefer entrances that aren't visible from the street. Side gates and back doors are exactly those spots, so they deserve coverage just as much as the front.

First-floor windows

Ground-level windows out of street view are a favorite. You don't need a camera on every window, but one covering a cluster of vulnerable ones is smart.

Common placement mistakes

  • Mounting too high. Great for overview, useless for identifying faces. Balance the two.
  • Shooting into the sun. East/west-facing cameras can wash out at sunrise/sunset — angle to avoid direct glare.
  • Ignoring Wi-Fi range. Wireless cameras at the edge of your property often drop. Plan for signal or go wired.
  • Leaving cables exposed. A snipped wire defeats the camera. Runs should be tucked and protected.

A quick rule of thumb

Cover every entry first (doors, gates, garage), then every approach (driveway, walkways), then valuables. If a stranger can't get onto your property without crossing at least one camera's view, you're in good shape.
