Cloud vs. Local Storage for Security Footage: Which Is Better?

Where your footage is stored affects cost, reliability, and how safe your evidence is. Both cloud and local storage have real advantages, and the best systems often use both.

Local storage (NVR/DVR)

Footage records to a recorder on-site. It's a one-time cost with no monthly fee, keeps lots of footage, and works even if your internet drops. The downside: if a thief takes the recorder, the footage goes with it.

Cloud storage

Footage uploads off-site, so it survives even if hardware is stolen or damaged, and it's easy to access anywhere. The trade-offs are monthly fees and reliance on your internet upload speed.

Why hybrid wins

  • Local keeps continuous, high-resolution footage affordably.
  • Cloud backs up key clips so evidence can't be physically stolen.
  • Together they cover each other's weak spots.

We'll recommend the right mix for your priorities and budget so your footage is both affordable and safe.
